Dump Messagebox

When the target process has reached the assumed OEP of the original application, a messagebox appears prompting you to save "OK" or "Cancel" the operation. If you don't wish to dump the target process (i.e. you are detaching, analyzing or logging nanomites) or have already dumped the file, press "Cancel".

When you press "OK", you will be prompted with a save file dialog. The target process is dumped to disk. At this point, no imports have been resolved. They will automatically be rebuilt after you save the dumped file.

*Very Important. Pay attention to the warning messages displayed in this messagebox. In most cases, you will see the message above, "No warnings issued", but for dll files and many Delphi applications that have an .itext section in the PE header, you will get warnings indicating that the OEP was not called directly from the Armadillo VM (see the picture below). For dll's, you can simply ignore these messages, but for other applications, you may have to either select ignore 2nd text section or bypass 2nd text section depending on the program and the inclusion of copymemII functionality. See these 2 options separately for more specific information on their use and functionality.
